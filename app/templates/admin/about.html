Ask Your Peeps started as a class project for Harvard's free online intro to computer science class.

In our HOA’s facebook page, I noticed that our neighbors were asking the same questions over and over again (i.e. which plumber or electrician do you recommend? Who do you recommend as baby or pet sitter?). With websites like Yelp, Angie’s List, or Google Maps, where its easy to look up 4 or 5 star rated providers, this would seem unnecessary.

What were our people looking for that these sites couldn't provide? I believe the answer is trust. Who provided the reviews? Were they compensated to provide a good review? Are they a serial complainer or do they give 5 stars to everything?

Asking your friends or neighbors is one solution for this problem. You know exactly who is giving the recommendation and if you know them well, you know whether you can trust them. The problem with this however is that it's inefficient and loses a lot of great information. Ask Your Peeps seeks to solve this problem by providing increased transparency in the source of reviews. We allow a user to filter reviews to only include businesses reviewed by their social circle (i.e. friends or members of groups to which they belong to). In these cases, we also show the full name of the person providing the review and ability to see their other reviews.

This is still very much a work in progress. We hope that you find Ask You Peeps to be useful. Please don’t hesitate to send any suggestions or feedback that you have.
